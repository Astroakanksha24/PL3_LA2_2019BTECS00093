About My City

Akola is located north-central of Maharashtra state, western India, on the banks of the Morna River. Also known as the ‚ÄòCotton City of India‚Äô due to its high cotton production, Akola is famous for its various shimmering rivers, gorgeous winters, sprawling forts, and intricately carved temples and mosques. Akola is an important city due to its history, culture, politics, and agriculture. After Nagpur and Amravati, Akola is the third largest city in the eastern region of Maharashtra. It also has a prominent road and rail junction in the Tapti River valley that functions as a commercial trading center. Akola is an important educational center with several colleges affiliated with the Sant Gadge Baba Amravati University. The city is developing into a market center. The primary language spoken by the people of Akola is Marathi; some communities also speak Urdu and Hindi.
